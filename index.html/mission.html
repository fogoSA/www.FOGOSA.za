THE FORGOTTEN NATION OF SOUTH AFRICA

    Our vision

    : A community where every 35 years and older person leads a dignified, healthy and secure life.

    Our mission

    : To promote the wellbeing, rights and inclusion of over 35 and older people so we can all enjoy a future free from poverty, inequality and discrimination.

    OUR DEMAND

    We demand the establishment of a Labour Desk which must be legislated will act as our point of reference and document all South African citizens above the age of 35 years with their skills and qualifications. This will assist the government to easier identify individuals for employment purposes.

    The purpose of the creation of the Labour Desk is:

    •To create a caring, protective and enabling environment for addressing issues of unemployed people of over 35 years of age. These issues will include employment references as most members of this age group no longer have references, due to having been long idle. On behalf of our membership, we will also campaign for exemption in regard to the credit bureau as most have been off and on had temporary jobs and that type of employment has affected their credit ratings

    •To promote Human Rights for persons who have been prejudiced and becomes victims of work-related violence/abuse because of their age.

    •To provide professional mental health services and any other services conducive to recovery, to all persons that have at places of work be subjected to prejudice and discrimination based on their age.

    •To stimulate the development and improve the quality of life skills and Skills Retraining Programmes in partnership with relevant organisations.

    •To provide guidance and work in collaboration with accredited professional departments and agencies and ensure high-quality services to job seekers of over 35 years who wish to enter the job market.

    •To provide information, creating public awareness in the broader community in respect of the discrimination faced by persons older than 35 years in job-related matters in workplaces, stressing their Human Rights and the gender-based violence/abuse they may experience.

    •To engage in collaborative relationships with relevant partners for ongoing service and developmental research.
